FAQ

Questions about the Tongue Scraper

Quick answers to help you get the best results.

What is a tongue scraper and why use one? +

A tongue scraper removes the bacteria-laden coating that builds up on the tongue surface overnight. This coating is a primary source of bad breath. Scraping is more effective at removing it than brushing alone.

Is stainless steel better than plastic for tongue scrapers? +

Yes. Stainless steel is more hygienic (non-porous), more durable (won't flex or crack), and lasts indefinitely. Plastic scrapers degrade over time and need regular replacement.

How often should I use a tongue scraper? +

Daily use is recommended — ideally each morning before brushing your teeth. 2–3 gentle strokes from back to front is sufficient.

How do I clean the tongue scraper? +

Rinse thoroughly under running water after each use. For a deeper clean, it can go in the dishwasher. The stainless steel won't rust or corrode.

Will it cause a gag reflex? +

Start closer to the middle of the tongue and gradually work further back as you get comfortable. Most people adapt quickly within the first few uses.

Does it come in plastic-free packaging? +

Yes. The tongue scraper ships in recyclable kraft paper packaging with no plastic wrap, no plastic inserts, and no unnecessary materials.
